What does Occupational Health and Safety mean?

Occupational Health and Safety is a practice and a field of study aimed to reinforce health and safety standards in the workplace. It is recognized as a formal strategy to prevent illnesses, accidents, and fatalities in the educational workplace as well as in other industries. It promotes continuous awareness, safety procedures, precautions, and health guidelines.

As a practice, in educational context, it aims to protect students from learning environment hazards, prevent illnesses, accidents, and fatalities, and ensure the safety, welfare, well-being, and academic engagement of all students and staff.

As a field of study, OHS is concerned with tracking work-related injuries and illnesses in the worker population, testing the effectiveness of risk mitigation measures, and assessing emerging safety risks.
